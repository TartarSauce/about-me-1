    Work Bio

    I gradulated from high school early when I was 16 years old and started to work in the maritime industry when I was 17. My first adventure on in this field was commercial diving.

    Commercial Diving: I went to commercial diving school when I was 17 and started diving commercially shortly there after. I worked for a tanker barge company doing hull inspection and prop shaft repairs. I then moved to above water activities.

    Marine Surveying: I started in the marine surveying business when I was 19. This job involved auditing cargo shipments and calclating manifests. This job took me to my next adventure, merchant marine.

    Merchant Marine: I worked locally as an Able Body Seaman on Tug Boats. This involved making and breaking barge tows and assisting vessels into ports. I worked on board a manned tug boat for 6 weeks on and 4 weeks off. Then I got married. Hello shore.

    Barge Tankerman: This was a shoreside job which intailed working still on the water but on a job to job circumstance. It involved a lot of travel where I was often flown into remote places to load and unload tank barges. Time to go into the office.

    Marine Vessel Operations: I worked as an operations manager dispatching and crewing vessels. I made sure everyone got to their job in one piece and got home the same. In the process I work with a lot of major oil refiners. As such I took advantage of training and instruction that moved me into a lab.

    Lab Time: I worked as a marine fuel specialist responsible for transfering petroleum in pipelines, tanker trucks, tanker barges, tanker ships and shoreside storage tanks. I then tested this material to maintain a quality product. This experience lent itself to the next stage....Marine cargo inspection.

    Marine Cargo Inspection: I worked for a company that audited the transfers of sales of petroleum products. This involved brokering the deal and set market price based on quality. This company was the desinated "middle-man" for huge oil companies that would buy and sell from each other but didn't trust each other.
